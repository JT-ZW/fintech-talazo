Frequently Asked Questions

How is the financial health score calculated?

The financial health score is calculated using a combination of soil quality metrics, farming experience, land area, crop selection, and historical performance data. The algorithm weighs these factors to provide a score between 0-100.

What soil parameters are most important for loan approval?

Key soil parameters include pH level (6.0-7.5 optimal), nitrogen content, phosphorus and potassium levels, organic matter percentage, and moisture content. These directly impact crop yield potential and loan risk assessment.

How often should soil tests be conducted?

We recommend soil testing every 6 months or at least once per growing season. More frequent testing (monthly) may be beneficial for precision agriculture and optimal loan terms.

What happens if a farmer defaults on a loan?

Our system includes early warning indicators and support mechanisms. We work with farmers to restructure payments, provide additional training, or adjust farming strategies before considering default procedures.

How do I export data and reports?

Use the export buttons in the Reports section or individual farmer profiles. Data can be exported in CSV, PDF, or Excel formats. Ensure you have appropriate permissions for data access.
