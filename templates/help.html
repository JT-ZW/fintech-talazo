Frequently Asked Questions

The financial health score is calculated using a sophisticated algorithm that combines multiple factors including soil quality metrics (pH level, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium content), farming experience, land area size, crop selection diversity, historical performance data, and market conditions. Each factor is weighted based on its impact on agricultural success and financial stability. The algorithm provides a score between 0-100, where scores above 75 indicate excellent financial health and loan eligibility.

The most critical soil parameters for loan approval include: pH level (optimal range 6.0-7.5), nitrogen content (minimum 35mg/kg), phosphorus levels (minimum 15mg/kg), potassium content (minimum 120mg/kg), organic matter percentage (minimum 2.5%), and soil moisture content (20-30% range). These parameters directly correlate with crop yield potential and therefore impact loan risk assessment. Higher values in these parameters result in better loan terms and higher approval amounts.

We recommend comprehensive soil testing every 6 months or at minimum once per growing season to maintain accurate financial assessments. For precision agriculture and optimal loan terms, monthly testing may be beneficial, especially during critical growing periods. Emergency testing should be conducted after severe weather events, pest outbreaks, or significant changes in farming practices. Regular testing ensures real-time risk assessment and can help improve loan terms through demonstrated soil health improvement.

Our system includes comprehensive early warning indicators and farmer support mechanisms designed to prevent defaults. When risk indicators suggest potential payment difficulties, we immediately engage with farmers to provide assistance including: loan restructuring options, extended payment terms, additional agricultural training, farming strategy optimization, and connection to agricultural extension services. We work collaboratively to find sustainable solutions before considering any default procedures, as farmer success is our primary objective.

Data and reports can be exported using the export buttons located in the Reports section of the dashboard or within individual farmer profiles. Available formats include CSV (for spreadsheet analysis), PDF (for presentations and official documents), and Excel (for advanced data manipulation). Ensure you have appropriate user permissions for data access. Exported reports include soil analysis data, financial assessments, loan histories, and performance metrics. Bulk exports for multiple farmers can be requested through the system administrator.

AI crop recommendations are generated using machine learning algorithms that analyze soil conditions, climate data, market prices, and historical performance. Each recommendation includes a suitability percentage, expected yield estimates, optimal planting periods, and required soil amendments. Recommendations are ranked by profitability potential and risk factors. Higher percentage matches indicate better alignment with current soil and environmental conditions, leading to higher success probability and better loan terms.
